Market Signal

The Used 3-Row EV Market Is Softening

Across the board, pre-owned prices dropped — some dramatically. This isn’t a single-brand story; it’s structural. More supply from 2025–2026 model years, aggressive OEM incentives on new inventory, and the arrival of the IONIQ 9 and Getaway are all putting downward pressure on the used market.

Fastest Depreciation

The Volvo EX90 is depreciating faster than any other luxury EV in the dataset. A 2025 Twin Motor Plus that stickered around $80K is now going for $47–55K used — a $25K+ haircut in roughly twelve months.

Price Collapse

Kia EV9 2026: Used Below MSRP — Already

The 2026 Kia EV9 is trading at 35–45% off MSRP on the used market, just months after going on sale. A GT-Line that listed at $71,900 new is now going for $40–49K used. Aggressive dealer discounting and Kia’s own price adjustments are flooding the market with cheaper new inventory, cratering residuals.

Range King

Lucid Gravity GT: 437 Miles EPA

The Lucid Gravity Grand Touring quietly became the longest-range 3-row EV by a wide margin. Its EPA-rated 437 miles is nearly 100 miles more than the next closest competitor — and Lucid raised the MSRP by $4K to $98,900, signaling confidence in the product.

Officially Named

Subaru Getaway: No Longer “3-Row EV”

Subaru unveiled the Getaway at the New York Auto Show — the official name for what we’ve been tracking as “Subaru 3-Row EV.” It shares Toyota’s e-TNGA platform with the Highlander EV and brings legit specs to the table.

  • 420 hp dual-motor AWD, sub-5-second 0–60
  • 95.8 kWh battery, 300+ miles range (mfr estimate)
  • 3,500 lb towing, 150 kW DC fast charge (10–80% in 30 min)
  • 7 seats with 15.9 cu ft behind 3rd row, 45.6 behind 2nd
  • NACS charging port, EyeSight driver assist
  • On sale late 2026 · Pricing TBD (mid-$40K expected)
