Pricing Correction

Kia EV9 2024: Mid-Tier Trims Held Value Better Than Expected

Three weeks ago we reported that the EV9 used market was collapsing. That held true for 2025–2026 models — but the 2024 cohort tells a different story. The 2024 Land and GT-Line are trading meaningfully higher than our previous estimates, suggesting buyers are distinguishing between model years more than we expected.

Spec Correction

Hyundai IONIQ 9: 350 kW, Not 233 kW

Our dataset had the IONIQ 9 pegged at 233 kW DC fast charging across all six trims. That was wrong. Hyundai’s official USA spec confirms 800V architecture with 350 kW peak charging — corrected across the full lineup.

350

All IONIQ 9 Trims (6 trims)

dc_fast_charge_kw: 233 kW → 350 kW  ·  800V platform, 10–80% in ~24 min

Why it matters: 350 kW puts the IONIQ 9 at the top of the charging speed leaderboard alongside the Lucid Gravity. This wasn’t a minor rounding error — 233 vs. 350 kW is a completely different road-trip experience.
Range Update

Lucid Gravity GT: Now 450 Miles EPA

We corrected the Gravity GT range to 437 mi just three weeks ago. It’s already been superseded: Lucid’s official site now shows 450 mi EPA for the Grand Touring. The Dream Edition holds at 420 mi.

450

Gravity Grand Touring

437 mi → 450 mi EPA  ·  Now 115+ miles ahead of the nearest 3-row rival

Context: The gap between Lucid and the rest of the 3-row field keeps widening. The next-closest competitor (Rivian R1S Quad) tops out around 321 mi. 450 mi is a different product category.
End of an Era

Tesla Model X: Discontinued

Tesla confirmed the Model X is being discontinued in Q2 2026. The vehicle that essentially invented the 3-row electric SUV — falcon-wing doors and all — won’t be sold new again. It moves from the comparison to a new section on the site: the Goodbyelist.

✕

Tesla Model X (2012–2026)

Final MSRPs: $99,990 base · $114,990 Plaid  ·  Inventory available while supplies last

What it means: If you’ve been watching Model X prices fall and considering one, this is likely as low as they’ll get while new. Once inventory clears, it’ll only exist in the used market — and a freshly discontinued vehicle with a loyal community tends to hold value better than normal depreciation would suggest. The Model X shaped every 3-row EV that came after it.
